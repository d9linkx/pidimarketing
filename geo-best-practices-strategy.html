Most marketers approach Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) by chasing prompts. They ask, "What are people typing into ChatGPT?" and then build content around those queries. In 2026, this is a losing strategy. AI search engines don't match prompts to words; they match intent to entities. If you aren't the recognized entity for a solution, volume won't save you.

1. The Prompt Volume Fallacy

Definition

Prompt Chasing is the reactive practice of creating content based on high-frequency search strings without establishing a underlying semantic connection to the brand's core authority.

Volume is a vanity metric in AI search. Because generative responses are synthesized, one highly-authoritative piece of content can satisfy 10,000 different prompt variations. Focusing on the "words" in a prompt is like focusing on the color of a car's paint when the engine is what matters.

2. Mapping Entity Intent

Instead of prompt volume, we look at Entity Connectivity. How closely does your brand (the entity) relate to the solution the user seeks?

Strategy Focus AI Impact
Prompt-Based Specific Keywords Low; easily summarized and ignored.
Entity-Based Subject Authority High; cited as the "Source of Truth."
Key Takeaway: Stop chasing the "how" people ask, and start dominating the "what" they are asking about. Authority is the only currency AI search accepts.
